Vacancies

Global Biotech Revolution (GBR) is currently looking for a highly motivated biotech enthusiast to lead the executive team of GapSummit 2016 in the role of President of GapSummit 2016.

Organisation description

GBR is a student-led not-for-profit organisation based in Cambridge, UK. GBR’s mission is to inspire young people and support the next generation of leaders who will ensure the sustainability and growth of biotechnology. We do this by connecting biotech think-tanks, industrial leaders, policy makers and research pioneers to Leaders of Tomorrow in biotechnology through online engagement and conferences.

GBR’s flagship event is the GapSummit- the world’s FIRST inter-generational leadership summit in biotechnology. At GapSummit 2014, 100 Leaders of Tomorrow in biotechnology from 35 nations were selected and sponsored to attend the world-class 3-day summit.

Opportunities

The successful candidate will be the heart of GapSummit 2016. The President, together with Vice President and Chief Operating Officer, will:

  • Create, shape and mould the next global inter-generational leadership meeting in biotechnology
  • Appoint their remaining GapSummit team members
  • Have an amazing opportunity to be in contact with biotech and bio-pharma industry executives, policy makers and research pioneers from leading institutions
  • Connect with the Leaders of Tomorrow in biotechnology from across the world

Role descriptions

From day one your prime responsibility will be to organise the GapSummit for 2016.

The open position requires long term motivation and a high and constant time commitment. The role involves planning, written and oral presentations, managing and executing tasks in the fields of finance, team and speaker recruitment, delegate management, legal contracts and many more.

The key for a successful application are team leading capabilities, excellent presentation skills, a high level of organisational skills and the motivation to familiarise yourself with complex and unknown topics.

The President will report directly to the GBR Advisory Board and negotiate with funding bodies.

The President will be able to build your own team and manage your own resources.

The commitment to the project and the team will involve approximately 10-15 hours of time per week with peak times prior and during the GapSummit 2016. Furtheron you will be held responsible for the handover to the next team.

Specific role descriptions

GapSummit President:

  • Develops GapSummit vision and goals
  • Inspires and motivates the team
  • Coordinates recruitment of speakers
  • Supports the Vice Presidentwith fund raising and sponsors reports
  • Supervises Advisory Board meetings and reports

Application process

The application process consists of three stages

  • A written application
  • Followed by a 15 minute interview
  • Finally a discussion/presentation of your vision for GapSummit 2016

Written applications should be submitted by 10 PM GMT, 21st of November 2014. Applications from Cambridge UK residents are preferred but not required. You are required to be a registered student or post-doctoral researcher at least until summer 2016.

Your application involves

  • CV, not to exceed 2 pages
  • Proof of student or post-doctoral status
  • Answers to the following questions (max. 150 words per question):
    • What is your motivation for applying to your chosen position and to organise GapSummit 2016?
    • Why are you the best candidate for your chosen position?
    • How would you enhance the experience of delegates and speakers at the GapSummit 2016?
